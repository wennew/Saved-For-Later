Projection of Summer Precipitation Over the Southeastern U.S. in the Super-Parameterized CCSM4

JC by Xiaojie Zhu et al, 2015-08-18

Projection of Summer Precipitation Over the Southeastern U.S. in the Super-Parameterized CCSM4

Xiaojie Zhu1,* and Cristiana Stan1,2,+

1 Center for Ocean-Land-Atmosphere Studies, George Mason University, Fairfax, Virginia, USA

2 Department of Atmospheric, Oceanic and Earth Sciences, George Mason University, Fairfax, Virginia, USA

Projections of the hydrological cycle over the Southeastern U.S. are compared between CCSM4 and SP-CCSM4. Under the extreme forcing of the RCP8.5 climate change scenario, in Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Kentucky the super-parameterized model, SP-CCSM4, projects a decrease in the summer precipitation whereas the conventionally parameterized CCSM4 projects an increase in the summer rainfall. The projected reduction in summer precipitation in SP-CCSM4 is due to the remote influence from the northwest intrusion of the North Atlantic subtropical high, as well as the local decrease of soil moisture content. Both models show that summer precipitation over the Southern U.S. is characterized by a positive feedback with soil moisture. However, in CCSM4 rainfall increases with increasing soil moisture and in SP-CCSM4 rainfall decreases with decreasing soil moisture.

The different representation of cloud processes in the two models yields different responses of precipitation to the latent heat flux changes over the Southeastern U.S. Moreover, multivariate EOF analyses in the two models suggest that the local land-atmosphere interactions have a stronger influence on the projected changes of precipitation over the Southeastern U.S. than the North Atlantic subtropical high does.
