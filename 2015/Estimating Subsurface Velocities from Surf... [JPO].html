Estimating Subsurface Velocities from Surface Fields with Idealized Stratification

JPO by J. H. LaCasce et al, 2015-07-23

Estimating Subsurface Velocities from Surface Fields with Idealized Stratification

J. H. LaCasce*

University of Oslo

J. Wang

Scripps Institute of Oceanography

The method of Wang et al. (2013), for predicting subsurface velocities and density from sea surface buoyancy and surface height, is extended by incorporating analytical solutions to make the vertical projection. One solution employs exponential stratification and the second has a weakly stratified surface layer, approximating a mixed layer. The results are evaluated using fields from a numerical simulation of the North Atlantic. The simple exponential solution yields realistic subsurface density and vorticity fields to nearly 1000 m depth. Including a mixed layer improves the response in the mixed layer itself, and hence at high latitudes where the mixed layer is deeper. It is in the mixed layer that the surface quasi-geostrophic (SQG) approximation is most applicable. Below that the first baroclinic mode dominates, and that mode is well-approximated by one obtained with exponential stratification.
